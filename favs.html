my favorite movies ever
the lost boys

you probably know this movie already, which makes you a lucky guy. god i love this movie. this movie is beautiful, the soundtrack, the set and colors, the minutiae. it also has some great themes despite the era-typical corniness. david is so me. go watch this now if you've yet to.
how i learned to stop worrying and love the bomb

i know, kubrick, but seriously. this movie changed how i approached a lot of shit. it started because i was curious about where the saying 'love the bomb' came from and what it meant, and it just so happens its origin is a kickass movie. awesome. i love the title so much. it means a million different things to me. when you see this movie it'll also mean something to you.
children shouldn't play with dead things

found on accident, and the title compelled me once again. i've loved it for years. it's so seventies and so unfunny, charming despite that fact. there's a scene near the end where that zombie is on the bed that stuck with me. i feel like it has something important to say about consent, too, but maybe i'm crazy. rad film though.
meat rack (1970)

holy jesus. this one's like huge queer history but super unknown despite that, it's definitely not something to watch with a parent or maybe really anyone else, but it's probably the best artsy movie i've ever seen. i love every part of it and everything it tries to tell you. it's also only 5% as internally homophobic as it seems.
